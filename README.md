# Adecco India: HR Analytics & Attrition Case Study  
**Developed by:** Adarsh Dwivedi | **Role:** Data & Business Analyst  

---

##  Project Overview  
This project analyzes workforce data from **Adecco India (Software Development Company)** to uncover the drivers behind rising attrition rates, especially in the **Sales Department** and **Junior-level staff**.  
Using **Excel (Power Query, Pivot Tables, Advanced Formulas)**, raw HR data was transformed into actionable insights to help management reduce turnover and improve employee satisfaction.

##  Data Processing & Excel Techniques  
- **Data Cleaning:** Power Query used to handle blank cells & duplicates  
- **Feature Engineering:** Created new columns (Age Group, Salary Slab) for deeper analysis  
- **Formula Mastery:** Applied VLOOKUP, INDEX-MATCH, Nested IF for metric calculations  
- **Pivot Tables:** Department-wise & Gender-wise attrition analysis  
- **Scatter Plot & Trendlines:** Showed impact of *Years Since Last Promotion* on *Performance Rating*

##  Detailed Insights  
- **High-Risk Group:** Sales department junior-level employees (Job Level 1 & 2) show highest attrition  
- **Overtime Factor:** Employees with regular overtime have 30% higher turnover probability  
- **The 2-Year Gap:** Lack of promotion for 2+ years leads to declining performance (negative trendline)  
- **Travel Stress:** Frequent travelers show higher burnout & attrition  
- **Income vs Retention:** $2,000–$4,500 monthly income bracket has peak turnover  

---

##  Strategic Recommendations  
- **Retention Bonus:** Introduce performance-based bonus for Sales team after 1-year completion  
- **Career Pathing:** Define clear promotion timelines for junior roles  
- **Travel Optimization:** Provide remote work or compensatory off for frequent travelers  
- **Engagement:** Conduct HR-led feedback sessions in low satisfaction departments
